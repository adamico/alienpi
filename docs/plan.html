Game Design Document — Session Notes

ECON-SHMUP

An economy-driven shoot-em-up prototype
ENGINE Custom JS / WebGL
SCOPE Solo Dev / Vertical Slices
INSPIRED BY Eve Online · C64 Ghostbusters

Core Concept

A classic shoot-em-up where the meta-game is an economic survival loop. You start in debt, buy a ship, fly combat missions to earn money, pay repair costs, and gradually unlock manufacturing and automation. Inspired by Eve Online's player-driven economy, compressed into a shmup format.

The score system becomes the currency. Every kill, every dodge, every pickup earns in-game ISK — not just pride points.

Core Game Loop — MVP

Take Loan
→
Buy Ship
→
Fly Mission
→
Earn Money
→
Pay Repairs
→
Choose Next Run

After each mission, the player sees their ammo spent and hull damage taken, then faces a repair bill. The question is: can you afford to keep flying? That financial pressure replaces lives and continues as the tension engine.

Existing Foundation

The prototype builds on an existing boss-fight shmup with:

3 SHIPS
Each starts with 1 weapon. 2 additional weapons unlock via in-run power-up drops, each upgradable across 3 tiers
3-TIER POWER-UPS
Random loot drops in-run. Unlock AND upgrade weapons — no two runs identical
JUICY COMBAT
Particles, post-processing, WebGL — already polished
SCORE SYSTEM
Ready to convert directly into in-game currency

Development Phases

01
MVP — BUILD THIS FIRST
Loan → Fight → Repair
Convert score to Substrate. Add purification (repair) + ammo costs after each run. Tune the payout-vs-repair ratio until the loop feels right. Validate: do players want to grind the boss again for economic reasons?
DEATH MECHANIC
On ship destruction, all acquired Substrate is kept. Ship loss is penalty enough at this stage. Forgiving by design — keeps early players engaged with the loop without frustration spirals.
02
VERTICAL SLICE 2
Mission Board + Insurance
Multiple contract types with different payouts and risk levels. Player chooses based on available Substrate and ship condition. Difficulty affects reward.
SUBSTRATE ANCHOR DEVICE
A purchasable device bought before each run. On ship destruction, anchors acquired Substrate so Gaia can recover it. Two tiers: partial recovery (50%) or full recovery (100%) at higher cost. Forces a meaningful pre-run spending decision: safety net vs. maximum investment.
03
VERTICAL SLICE 3
Manufacturing
Option to source parts and build ships yourself, cheaper than buying off-the-shelf. Buy a workshop. Assemble components. Sell surplus to NPC market.
04
LATE GAME
Automation
Production lines run passively. Hire workers. Build passive income streams. Scale from lone pilot to industrial operation.

Tuning Knobs — Phase 1

The entire economy balance lives in these ratios. This is the primary design work of the MVP:

PAYOUT RATE
How much money per kill / per boss clear
REPAIR COST
Hull damage → credits. The core tension lever
AMMO COST
Resupply price between runs
LOAN PRESSURE
Repayment schedule — ticking clock or flexible?
If repairs cost more than you earn, you're in a death spiral. If repairs cost too little, there's no tension. The sweet spot is tight margins with room to improve through skill.

Open Questions

Q1
Full run required, or can the player abort mid-fight?
Option: allow retreat with partial earnings but still pay repair costs — preserves capital, sacrifices payout. Very Eve-like.
Q2
Is the current boss variety enough to sustain replays?
The random weapon unlock order already creates meaningful run variation — finding your best weapon early vs. late changes the power curve significantly. Test the economy loop first before adding procedural boss behavior. If players grind the same boss for money, variety is sufficient.
Q3
When does manufacturing unlock — time, milestone, or cash threshold?
Gating needs to feel like a natural opportunity, not a wall.
Q4
Should weapon unlocks ever become purchasable between runs?
Phase 1: weapons stay as pure in-run drops (random, free). Later phases: option to buy a guaranteed weapon unlock before a mission — trading money for reliability vs. gambling on drops. Interesting economy tradeoff.

Lore — The Corruption Cycle

An alien entity is invading Earth — not merely conquering it, but converting it. Earth's matter, energy, and living systems are being absorbed and transformed into something hostile and alien. A hero, chosen by the collective planetary consciousness Gaia, is the last line of resistance.

The Resource: Reclaimed Substrate

When you destroy alien ships, you don't earn money — you liberate corrupted Earth matter. Your vessel, being Gaia-attuned, passively purifies what it salvages during combat. Wreckage releases Reclaimed Substrate: recovered Earth essence freed from alien corruption.

RECLAIMED SUBSTRATE
The core resource. Harvested from enemy wreckage. Fuels all operations.
CORRUPTION TOLL
Your ship absorbs alien corruption during combat. Cleansing it between runs costs Substrate.
DIRECTED GAIA ENERGY
What ammunition actually is — purified Substrate channeled as weaponized Earth force.
GAIA ADVANCE
The "loan" — Gaia's collective fronts you initial purified reserves to begin the fight, trusting you to replenish them.

Economy Reframed Through Lore

→
Repairs = Purification
Your ship isn't just damaged — it's been contaminated by proximity to the alien entity. Cleansing costs Substrate.
→
Ammo resupply = Refined Substrate converted to Gaia energy
You're not buying bullets — you're converting recovered matter back into directed force.
→
Manufacturing (late game) = Purification infrastructure
You're not building a factory — you're constructing refinement networks that accelerate Earth's recovery.
The cycle is self-sustaining by design: destroy the alien, reclaim what was taken, use it to destroy more. You are not a mercenary. You are the mechanism of Earth's recovery.

Design Philosophy

Build vertically. Validate each layer before adding the next. One complete, playable loop is worth more than four half-built systems. The economy loop is the game — combat is the input mechanism.
