Today I decided to reckon with the drafted economy system for AOA.

The economy system that's currently in the game comes from an idea I had early on: a shmup is about shooting things, sometimes a lot of things with a lot of bullets. If the player had to think about funding each shot and each lost ship, it would add a layer of strategy and resource management to the game.

I also developed a general lore for the game which may require a separate devlog entirely to itself, but the gist of it is that the player is part of a defense force of a civilization which is being menaced by an invading alien force. The alien force corrupts the planet resources, air, water, minerals, etc. This corruption, the rot, has triggered the immunitary response of the planet which manifests itself as the Gaia entity.

I also drew inspiration from the old Ghostbusters game of the 80s, where the player was given a bank loan to fund their ghostbusting business, and each ghost caught would earn them money, but each trap set, life lost would cost them money. At the end of the game, if the player had paid back their loan, they would be allowed to finish the game, otherwise they would lose.

Starting from these three ideas, I drafted a system where the player has been given a budget of substrate, the pure and uncorrupted resources of the planet, to fund their defense efforts. Each shot fired and each ship lost would cost substrate, and the player would earn substrate by defeating enemies. Each enemy defeated would allow the player to regain a certain amount of substrate.

I then added a lore screen explaining the history of the conflict and the current state of the world, and how the player fits into it. I also added screens showing the initial budget, current substrate amounts, enemy payouts and repair costs.

Long term, I wanted a factory system where players process raw substrate into usable fuel for repairs and upgrades. This adds another strategic layer: balance immediate needs against long-term economy building.

I got too focused on making the main loop feel satisfying, which consumed time and pushed the economy system aside.

Finally, I took a step back and parked the economy system for now to focus on arcade mode, which is the core experience that needs to work first.

What the game lacked was a proper score system, which I built today. The score system has two parts: a proximity multiplier that rewards aggressive play by increasing points based on how close you are to enemies, and boss HP milestones that give bonuses for precise execution at 75%, 50%, and 25% health thresholds.

I thought about adding a chain multiplier, which would increase the score earned from defeating enemies in quick succession, but I decided to scrap it because the boss design doesn't create enough kill opportunities.

Did I make the right call parking the economy system, or will I regret abandoning it later?
